Thursday October 20, 2005

There’s this guy here who calls me “Earl.” He says, “Hey Earl.” So I took to calling him “Earl” also. At first I thought his name was Jimmy, but it’s Matt. Everyone calls him Willie, except me, I call him “Earl.” He doesn’t like it I don’t think. Also I hit him in the cheek with a rubber band by mistake, so maybe that’s what he didn’t like. Probably. But what does “Earl” mean? Earl: n. A British nobleman next in rank below a marquis. Marquis, Don: n. Humorist who wrote about the imaginary life of cockroaches. Cockroach: n. Formed from the two words: cock, the word for rooster, and roach, the name of a fish. Literally: roosterfish. So there you go. Summary of my day: * * * * * * * *. An 8-star day. Out of 9.
